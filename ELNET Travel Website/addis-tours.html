Addis Ababa City Tours

Immerse yourself in Africa's diplomatic capital. Explore historic cathedrals, museums, local markets, and modern parks.

City Tour Packages

Click a package to reveal details, museum spots, and highlights. All packages feature premium travel amenities.

Package One Historic Museums & Cathedral Trail

A deep historical dive into the spiritual and royal history of Ethiopia's capital, ending with a bustling local market experience.

Saint George Cathedral

Historic octagonal cathedral built in 1896 to commemorate the Battle of Adwa. Houses an excellent ecclesiastical museum.

National Palace / Jubilee Palace Museum

Former royal palace of Emperor Haile Selassie, housing historic royal furniture, vehicles, and diplomatic gifts.

Holy Trinity Cathedral

The highest ranking Orthodox cathedral in Addis, final resting place of Haile Selassie and patriots of WWII.

Shola Market

A lively local market known for traditional spices, grains, and typical Addis household items.

Package Two Entoto Mountain Adventure & Cultural Market

Combine high-altitude views and pine forest recreation with shopping for traditional woven fabrics.

Entoto Park

Perched at 3,200m, offering scenic views, walking tracks, and optional adventure activities (archery, paintball, zip line, go-kart, horse riding at client's own cost).

Shiro Meda Market

The ultimate market destination in Addis Ababa for buying beautiful, hand-woven traditional Ethiopian cotton clothing (Habesha Kemis).

Package Three Unity Park & Mercato Expedition

Experience the modern renewal of Ethiopia's seat of power and the largest open-air market in Africa.

Unity Park

Located inside the Grand Palace. Features historic banquet halls, regional cultural pavilions, zoo, and beautiful botanical setups.

Addis Mercato

Africa's largest open-air market, a bustling labyrinth offering everything from coffee and spices to metalworks and local clothing.

Book Package Three
Package Four Paleoanthropology & Historical Entoto Royal Trail

Visit the origins of humanity and trace the original royal settlement of Emperor Menelik II on the hills of Entoto.

National Museum of Ethiopia

Home of the famous fossilized hominid skeleton 'Lucy' (Dinknesh), alongside pre-Aksumite archaeological displays.

Ethnographic Museum

Housed inside Haile Selassie's former palace at Addis Ababa University, featuring rich tribal art and musical instruments.

Entoto Saint Mary Church & Menelik Palace

The site where Menelik II was crowned, alongside the ruins of his original stone palace and Saint Raguel Church.

Package Five Adwa Museum & Traditional Crafts Center

Celebrate African victory and learn about ancient Ethiopian pottery and handicraft traditions.

Saint George Cathedral

Iconic octagonal cathedral and museum highlighting historical tapestries and crowns.

Adwa 00 Museum

A magnificent modern facility dedicated to the Battle of Adwa, symbolizing African victory over colonial powers.

Ensira Pottery Center

Discover the ancient clay pottery-making techniques preserved and practiced by local Ethiopian women artisans.

Shola Market

Explore the extensive spice lanes and authentic craft sections in this bustling open market.
